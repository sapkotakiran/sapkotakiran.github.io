The Problem

Misinformation spreads faster than corrections. Manual fact-checking cannot scale to platform volume. A classifier reduces moderation load — with real consequences for public discourse.

My Approach

Built a full NLP preprocessing pipeline (tokenisation → stopword removal → stemming → TF-IDF), then benchmarked classical ML before exploring deep learning. Evaluated on F1-score, not just accuracy.

Trade-offs & Challenges

BiLSTM and BERT offered marginal accuracy gains at significant compute cost. For most deployments the LR + TF-IDF pipeline delivers near-identical performance at a fraction of inference time — a deliberate engineering decision.

Impact

~98% accuracy at scale = 1-2 false positives per 100 articles. For platform moderation teams processing 10K+ articles daily, this translates to 100+ correct editorial decisions daily. Reduces manual fact-checking overhead while maintaining trust. LR pipeline is production-ready; deep learning path documented for future iterations.

The Problem

Recruiters spend 6–7 seconds scanning a resume. Automating job role classification at scale reduces manual screening by orders of magnitude — enabling faster, more consistent shortlisting for high-volume pipelines.

My Approach

Started with XGBoost as a measurable baseline, then built a PyTorch BiLSTM with custom attention. The attention layer highlights tokens that most influence role prediction — making the model interpretable, not just accurate.

Before vs After

XGBoost baseline: 82% accuracy, no sequence modelling, no interpretability. BiLSTM + Attention: 86% accuracy, captures word-order context, attention weights provide explainability. Trade-off: higher inference compute, meaningfully better on long-form text.

Impact & Scale

86% accuracy on 25+ job categories. Automates screening of 100+ resumes per cycle — reduces recruiter time from 6–7 seconds to <1 second per candidate with confidence-ranked predictions. At enterprise scale: 1000 resumes/week = 99+ hours saved monthly. Outperforms XGBoost baseline by 4 percentage points — a meaningfully better triage tool.

How I Think

From Data to Decision

Every system I build follows this deliberate workflow — not a checklist, but a thinking framework.

01
Understand the Problem

Define success metrics before touching data. What decision will this model enable? What does failure cost?

02
Data & EDA

Explore distributions, identify imbalances, find signal. Bad data decisions here ruin every step after.

03
Baseline First

Ship a simple model fast. Establishes a benchmark all complex models must beat.

04
Iterate & Evaluate

Beyond accuracy: precision, recall, F1, ROC-AUC. Choose the metric that matches the cost of error in context.

05
Decision & Handoff

Document trade-offs. Package for use. A model no one can run or explain is not a finished system.
